1. Purpose

This tool quantifies Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT) need using suspected malaria cases as a proxy. Because every suspected case is tested with an RDT (or microscopy), the number of suspected cases is a close approximation of the number of tests required. The tool estimates the typical suspected caseload per quarter for each district and nationally, from historical monthly reporting, and presents it as the quantification for the coming year.

2. How the estimate is built

The example below follows one facility, Kambia CHC (Kambia district), for the years 2024 and 2025.

  1. Fetch monthly data. For every Level-5 facility, pull the monthly suspected malaria counts for each selected year from the DHIS2 HMIS.
    Example: for Kambia CHC the tool pulls its 12 monthly suspected values for 2024, and again for 2025.
  2. Combine age categories. Each data element stores counts by age group; these are summed into a single total. No age breakdown is used in this tool.
    Example: in Jan 2024 the facility recorded 40 (0-59m) + 30 (5-14y) + 30 (15+y) = 100 suspected at the facility.
  3. Combine sources. Facility and Community counts are added together (or one source is used alone, depending on the Source setting) to give one monthly suspected total per facility.
    Example: that same January the community (HTR/ETR) reported 20, so the Combined monthly total = 100 + 20 = 120.
  4. Correct outliers. Extreme monthly spikes are capped at the 99.75th percentile per facility per year so a single reporting error does not distort the estimate. Zeros stay zero and missing months stay missing.
    Example: if Aug 2024 was keyed as 2,500 (a typo) while the facility usually sees about 150, it is pulled down to the facility's cap for that year (say 320). A month recorded as 0 stays 0; a blank month stays blank.
  5. Assign months to quarters. Q1 = Jan-Mar, Q2 = Apr-Jun, Q3 = Jul-Sep, Q4 = Oct-Dec.
    Example: January, February and March 2024 all belong to Q1 2024; the same months of 2025 belong to Q1 2025.
  6. Pool the monthly values. For each quarter, gather every monthly value from that quarter across all selected years into one set (for example, Q1 pools all Jan, Feb and Mar values from every year).
    Example: Q1 for this facility pools Jan/Feb/Mar of both years — 120, 140, 110 (2024) and 160, 130, 150 (2025) → the set {110, 120, 130, 140, 150, 160}.
  7. Take the central value. Compute the mean (default) or median of that pooled set. This is the typical monthly caseload for the quarter.
    Example: the mean of {110,120,130,140,150,160} = 135. So a typical Q1 month is 135 suspected cases, i.e. about 135 RDTs per month.
  8. Scale to a quarter. Quarterly estimate = typical monthly caseload × 3 months.
    Example: Q1 quarterly estimate = 135 × 3 = 405 RDTs.
  9. Annual estimate. Sum of the four quarterly estimates.
    Example: if Q1 = 405, Q2 = 360, Q3 = 300, Q4 = 330, the annual estimate = 1,395 RDTs for this facility for the coming year.
  10. Aggregate. For District and National views, facility monthly totals are summed first, then steps 5 to 9 are applied to the combined series.
    Example: to get the Kambia district figure, every facility's monthly totals in Kambia are added month by month, then steps 5 to 9 are run on that district series. National does the same across all facilities.

4. Facility coverage

Facility coverage numbers (registry total, active, non-reporting, in view) appear here after you run a quantification.

Only standard health facility types are counted (Hospital, Clinic, CHC, CHP, MCHP). Active means the facility reported at least one suspected value (Facility or Community) in the selected years. Facilities with no report in the selected years are counted in the registry total but do not contribute to the estimate.

6. Worked example

One facility, source Facility + Community, method Median, years 2024 and 2025.
Q1 monthly suspected values — 2024: Jan 120, Feb 140, Mar 110; 2025: Jan 160, Feb 130, Mar 150.
Pooled Q1 set: 110, 120, 130, 140, 150, 160.
Median (typical Q1 month) = (130 + 140) / 2 = 135.
Q1 quarterly estimate = 135 × 3 = 405.
Repeat for Q2, Q3 and Q4, then Annual estimate = Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4 estimates.
If Mean were selected instead: mean of the same set = 135 as well here, but the two methods differ whenever the values are skewed by a high or low month.

7. Assumptions and notes

Missing months are excluded from the pool rather than counted as zero, so reporting gaps do not drag the estimate down.
With two years selected, each quarter pools up to six monthly values. Adding more years produces a more stable median or mean.
Median is the default because malaria surveillance data often contain occasional spikes. Use Mean when a straight average is preferred, for example for very stable, high-volume facilities.
The same definitions and steps are written to the Method sheet of the Excel download, and reproduced in the R and Python scripts.

8. Technical steps (data flow)

This is the exact sequence the tool runs each time you click Fetch & Quantify. The example follows a run for years 2024 and 2025 (quantification for 2026).

  1. Read the inputs. Take From Year, To Year and the outlier setting, and build the year list. Example: 2024 to 2025 gives the years [2024, 2025]. The target year is the last year + 1, so 2026.
  2. Fetch the facility registry. Fetch all Level-5 organisation units and, for each one, read its id, name, and its District (Level 2) and Chiefdom (Level 4) parents. Keep the standard facility types only (Hospital, Clinic, CHC, CHP, MCHP). This gives the registry total. Any smaller unit below Level 5 is linked to its Level-5 parent so community reports can roll up.
  3. Fetch the monthly data, one request per year. For each year, fetch the suspected values for all 12 months at Level 5, asking for the six data value combinations (data element and age category):
    dx = FJafyE1kzs4.m5T8uBNB8aN ; FJafyE1kzs4.j7j6iaknOFn ; FJafyE1kzs4.zK1aiw8zFac ;
         wfaQBIVQxWo.NtjMVmRfvHH ; wfaQBIVQxWo.qDXW7WMLT99 ; wfaQBIVQxWo.mdjRPpvLKdu
    pe = 202401 ; 202402 ; … ; 202412  (then again 202501 … 202512)
    ou = LEVEL-5
    Each returned record is: data value, month, facility. Zero and empty values are dropped, and the facility is mapped to its Level-5 parent if needed.
  4. Correct outliers (if the setting is on). For every facility, year and each of the six values on its own, cap any month above the 99.75th percentile of that year. A 0 stays 0 and a missing month stays missing.
  5. Build monthly totals. For each facility, year and month, add the three facility age groups into a Facility total and the three community age groups into a Community total. A facility that has at least one value in the selected years is counted as active.
  6. Apply the source. Use Facility only, Community only, or Combined (Facility + Community, the default).
  7. Pool by quarter. Put each month in its quarter and gather all monthly values for that quarter across the years into one set. Example, facility X, Q1: Jan, Feb, Mar of 2024 and of 2025 = up to 6 values.
  8. Take the central value. Compute the mean (default) or median of that pooled set. This is the typical monthly caseload for the quarter.
  9. Scale to the estimate. Quarterly estimate = typical monthly caseload × 3. Annual estimate = Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4. This annual figure is the quantification for 2026.
  10. Aggregate. For District and National, add the facility monthly series together first, then repeat steps 7 to 9 on the combined series.
  11. Show and export. Fill the KPIs, charts and tables, and build the Excel and CSV files with the Facility, Community and Combined sheets.
Worked example through the whole flow — facility X, Combined, Mean, 2024 and 2025:
Step 3 returns the monthly suspected values. After adding facility + community age groups (steps 5 and 6), facility X has:
2024 Q1 months: Jan 120, Feb 140, Mar 110
2025 Q1 months: Jan 160, Feb 130, Mar 150
Step 7 pools Q1: 110, 120, 130, 140, 150, 160.
Step 8 mean = 135 (typical Q1 month).
Step 9 Q1 quarterly estimate = 135 × 3 = 405.
Repeat for Q2, Q3, Q4, then annual estimate = sum of the four = the 2026 quantification for facility X. Step 10 adds facility X into its district and national totals.
