What this tool does:
Estimates antimalarial treatment (ACT) need using the confirmed method. For each facility and month it computes adjusted confirmed cases = confirmed + TPR × max(treated − confirmed, 0), where TPR = confirmed ÷ tested. This releases the presumptively-treated cases (treated but never lab-confirmed) through the test positivity rate to estimate how many were truly malaria — the number of ACT courses required. It fetches confirmed, tested and treated (Facility + Community, all ages combined) per facility per month, corrects extreme outliers, then summarises the typical demand by quarter using the mean or median across the selected years \u2014 not a forecast for any single year.
Quarters: Q1 (Jan-Mar) · Q2 (Apr-Jun) · Q3 (Jul-Sep) · Q4 (Oct-Dec)
Quantification basis: For each facility & quarter, the mean (or median) of all monthly adjusted-confirmed values in that quarter, pooled across the selected years. This is the typical monthly ACT demand per quarter.

1. Purpose

This tool quantifies antimalarial treatment (ACT) need using the confirmed method. Every confirmed malaria case needs one ACT course, but some patients are treated presumptively without a confirming test. The confirmed method releases those presumptively-treated cases through the facility’s test positivity rate (TPR) to estimate how many were truly malaria, giving a fuller picture of treatment demand than raw confirmed counts. The result — adjusted confirmed cases — is summarised per quarter for each district and nationally as a typical-year demand profile — the mean or median of the observed years, not a projection for a specific year.

2. The adjustment

TPR = confirmed ÷ tested  ·  presumptive = max(treated − confirmed, 0)
adjusted confirmed = confirmed + TPR × max(treated − confirmed, 0)
The presumptively-treated group (treated but never lab-confirmed) is passed through the TPR to estimate how many would have tested positive, then added back to confirmed. When confirmed ≥ treated there is no presumptive group and adjusted confirmed = confirmed.

3. How the estimate is built

The example follows one facility, Kambia CHC, for 2024 and 2025.

  1. Fetch monthly data. For every Level-5 facility, pull monthly confirmed, tested and treated counts for each selected year from the DHIS2 HMIS.
    Example: for Kambia CHC the tool pulls its 12 monthly values for confirmed, tested and treated in 2024, and again in 2025.
  2. Combine age categories (children only). Each data element stores counts by age category option combo; the three age combos are summed into one total per measure. No age breakdown is used in this tool.
    Example: Jan 2024 confirmed = 18 (0-59m) + 12 (5-14y) + 10 (15+y) = 40.
  3. Assemble the three measures. Confirmed = microscopy-positive + RDT-positive (facility) and RDT-HTR-positive (community). Tested = positives + negatives. Treated = ACT <24h + ACT >24h. Facility and community are added together (or one source alone, per the Source setting).
    Example (Combined, Jan 2024): confirmed 40, tested 96, treated 60.
  4. Correct outliers. Each component is capped at the 99.75th percentile per facility per year so a single reporting spike does not distort the estimate. Zeros stay zero and missing months stay missing.
  5. Compute adjusted confirmed per month. Apply the adjustment to each month’s confirmed, tested and treated.
    Example: TPR = 40/96 = 0.417; presumptive = max(60−40,0) = 20; adjusted = 40 + 0.417×20 = 48.3 ACT courses for Jan.
  6. Assign months to quarters. Q1 Jan-Mar, Q2 Apr-Jun, Q3 Jul-Sep, Q4 Oct-Dec.
  7. Pool the monthly values. For each quarter, gather every monthly adjusted-confirmed value from that quarter across all selected years into one set.
  8. Take the central value. Mean (default) or median of the pooled set — the typical monthly ACT demand for the quarter.
  9. Scale to a quarter. Quarterly estimate = typical monthly demand × 3 months.
  10. Annual estimate. Sum of the four quarterly estimates — the typical-year ACT demand.
  11. Aggregate. For district and national views, facility monthly confirmed/tested/treated series are summed first, then the adjustment and steps 6–10 are applied to the combined series (so TPR reflects the aggregate).

7. Worked example

One facility, source Facility + Community, method Mean, years 2024 and 2025.
Per month the tool has confirmed, tested and treated, and computes adjusted confirmed. Suppose the adjusted-confirmed Q1 months are — 2024: 48, 56, 44; 2025: 64, 52, 60.
Pooled Q1 set: 44, 48, 52, 56, 60, 64.
Mean (typical Q1 month) = 54 ACT courses.
Q1 quarterly estimate = 54 × 3 = 162.
Repeat for Q2-Q4, then annual estimate = Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4 = the typical-year ACT demand.

8. Assumptions and notes

Missing months are excluded from the pool rather than counted as zero, so reporting gaps do not drag the estimate down.
The TPR is computed at the aggregation level being viewed: facility-level for facility rows, district totals for district rows, national totals for the TOTAL row.
When treated ≤ confirmed there is no presumptive uplift and adjusted confirmed equals confirmed.
One ACT course is assumed per adjusted confirmed case; apply age/weight-band formulation ratios downstream if procuring by pack size.
Test selector: choose RDT + Microscopy (default), RDT only, or Microscopy only. This sets which positives count as confirmed and which tests form the TPR denominator. Treated (ACT) is not recorded by diagnostic method, so in single-method mode the presumptive group (treated − confirmed) is larger; read RDT-only / Microscopy-only results as "adjusted to that method’s positivity". Community HTR/ETR performs RDT only, so Microscopy-only yields no community estimate.
